Antibiotic Resistance Crisis

The rise of antibiotic-resistant STIs (particularly gonorrhoea) threatens to make common infections untreatable. By 2050, antibiotic resistance could cost the NHS £3 billion annually - a 17x increase from today.

This isn't just a health crisis - it's an economic catastrophe in the making.

👥 Who's Most Affected?

Age Demographics

People aged 25-34 are most likely to attend sexual health services, but the crisis affects all age groups:

  • 15-24 year olds: Account for >50% of Chlamydia and Gonorrhoea cases in England (2020)
  • 25-34 year olds: Highest clinic attendance and STI diagnosis rates
  • All sexually active individuals: At increased risk due to exponentially rising case numbers

🚧 Why Current Methods Aren't Enough

Barriers to Existing Prevention

Condoms

  • ✅ Effective when used correctly and consistently
  • ❌ Reduced sensation complaints
  • ❌ Inconsistent use in long-term relationships
  • ❌ Breakage/slippage risks
  • ❌ Requires interruption of intimacy

PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)

  • ✅ Highly effective at preventing HIV
  • ❌ Doesn't protect against other STIs
  • ❌ Requires prescription and daily adherence
  • ❌ Not accessible to everyone
  • ❌ Limited awareness and uptake

Regular Testing

  • ✅ Identifies infections early
  • ❌ Doesn't prevent transmission
  • ❌ Requires clinic access and time
  • ❌ Many STIs are asymptomatic
  • ❌ Testing gaps leave windows of transmission

Vaccinations

  • ✅ Effective for HPV and Hepatitis B
  • ❌ Limited to specific pathogens
  • ❌ Declining uptake rates (68% drop in HPV vaccinations)
  • ❌ Doesn't cover most common STIs

💡 The Need for Innovation
