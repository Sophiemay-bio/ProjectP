🧪 Our Formulation

Three Pillars of Protection

1. Pathogen Binding Technology

Proprietary biomaterial formulation

Our patentable technology uses specialised compounds that:

  • Coat viral and bacterial surfaces - Creating a molecular barrier
  • Bind to pathogen proteins - Preventing host cell attachment
  • Neutralise infectivity - Reducing transmission risk
  • Work across pathogen types - Bacteria, viruses, and parasites

Mechanism of Action:

The active compounds in PHLYXR interact with surface proteins on STI pathogens, effectively "wrapping" them and preventing their ability to infect human cells. This physical barrier approach is fundamentally different from antibiotics, making it resistant to antimicrobial resistance.

2. pH Optimisation

Vaginal-matched pH 3.5-4.5

Maintaining optimal vaginal acidity:

  • Supports lactobacillus dominance - The cornerstone of vaginal health
  • Enhances natural defenses - Acidic environment inhibits pathogens
  • Prevents dysbiosis - No disruption to healthy microbiome
  • Reduces BV risk - Bacterial vaginosis prevention

Why pH Matters:

The healthy vaginal microbiome is dominated by lactobacillus species that produce lactic acid, maintaining pH 3.5-4.5. This acidic environment naturally inhibits many pathogens. Many lubricants disrupt this balance, PHLYXR preserves it.

3. Spermicidal Action

Dual-mechanism contraception

Non-hormonal pregnancy prevention through:

  • Acidic pH immobilisation - Sperm cannot survive in pH 3.5-4.5
  • Motility inhibition - Additional compounds prevent sperm movement
  • Membrane disruption - Affects sperm cell integrity
  • Immediate action - Protection from first application

Non-Hormonal Advantage:

Unlike hormonal contraceptives, PHLYXR provides contraception without systemic effects, making it suitable for those who cannot or prefer not to use hormonal methods.

❓ Scientific FAQs

Common Questions from Researchers and Healthcare Providers

Q: How does PHLYXR differ from Nonoxynol-9 spermicides?

A: Unlike N-9, which damages cell membranes indiscriminately (causing irritation and potentially increasing HIV transmission), PHLYXR uses targeted pathogen binding and maintains vaginal pH. Our formulation is designed to be non-irritating and microbiome-safe.

Q: Can pathogens develop resistance to PHLYXR?

A: The physical binding mechanism makes resistance development extremely unlikely. Unlike antibiotics that target specific metabolic pathways, PHLYXR physically coats pathogens, preventing attachment - a mechanism that cannot be easily circumvented through mutation.

Q: What is the mechanism for viral STI prevention?

A: PHLYXR binds to viral envelope proteins (for enveloped viruses like HSV and HIV) or capsid proteins (for non-enveloped viruses like HPV), preventing viral attachment to host cells and disrupting viral integrity.

Q: Is PHLYXR compatible with condoms?

A: Yes, PHLYXR is fully compatible with latex, polyisoprene, and polyurethane condoms. We recommend using both for maximum protection.

Q: What regulatory pathway are you following?

A: PHLYXR will be submitted to the MHRA (Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency) as a medical device with contraceptive and antimicrobial claims. We are following the appropriate regulatory framework for combination products.
